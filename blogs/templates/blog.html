About Me

Hello! I’m Ambrose Onyok, a 24-year-old Bachelor of Information Technology student at Muni University in Uganda. I am passionate about technology, coding, and helping communities understand the power of IT in their daily lives.

My IT Journey

From an early age, I have been fascinated by computers and how they work. Over the years, I have developed skills in:

  • Web Application Development (HTML, CSS, JavaScript)
  • Computer Repair and Assembly
  • Software Installation and Troubleshooting
  • Networking and IT Infrastructure
  • Teaching IT skills to peers and community members
"Technology is best when it brings people together." – My personal mantra in IT.

Community & Leadership

Aside from my academic work, I am actively involved in community projects. I have participated in IT sensitization programs, helping locals learn basic computer skills. I also served as the Council Speaker of Kaberamaido District, which strengthened my leadership and communication abilities.

Projects & Achievements

Some notable achievements and projects I have worked on include:

  • Developed a student portal web application for Muni University.
  • Organized community IT workshops that trained over 150 people.
  • Managed computer lab maintenance and software updates as an IT officer.
  • Received awards for outstanding communication and leadership skills.

My Vision

I aim to leverage IT skills to create innovative solutions that impact communities, improve education, and make technology accessible to everyone.
